Navient lawsuit claims weakened by borrower complaints

By: Charlotte Sutton

Trends in consumer complaints about consumer lending company Navient raises questions about the company’s claim about unsubstantiated lawsuits against the company.

The lawsuits, which forced the company to end their student loans services in September 2024, were filed in January 2017 and accused the company of several failures in lending student loans, including misleading their borrowers about income-driven repayments and into forbearance, a temporary agreement to pause or reduce loans.

According to the consumer compliance database from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) comparing Maryland and Missouri consumer complaints show a small uptick in complaints in 2024 following a large spike in complaints in 2017, with 292 filed complaints from Maryland consumers in 2024 and 191 complaints from Missouri consumers.

Complaints from consumers in these states show that they took issue with the services from the company’s employees, with both states’ main issue was dealing with Navient lenders and servicers.

The company has now been replaced with Aidvantage, which has been servicing loans owned by the U.S. Department of Education since December 2021.
